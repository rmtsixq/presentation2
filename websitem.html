Malware Presentation

Welcome to our presentation on malware. We'll discuss its definition, examples like ILOVEYOU and Koobface, and how to protect yourself.

What is Malware?

Malware is malicious software that harms your computer. Its goals include stealing information, damaging systems, or scamming users.

Examples: Viruses, worms, Trojan horses.

ILOVEYOU Virus (2000)

The ILOVEYOU virus spread through email attachments and caused billions of dollars in damages worldwide.

Impact: Crashed computers, deleted files, and spread globally in hours.

Koobface Worm (2008)

The Koobface worm spread through fake Facebook messages with malicious links. It targeted user data and promoted fake antivirus software.

Impact: Slower spread but financially scammed users worldwide.

Comparison: ILOVEYOU vs Koobface

ILOVEYOU spread faster and caused more direct damage, while Koobface was slower but focused on financial scams and data theft.

How to Protect Yourself

Follow these steps to stay safe:

  • Do not open unknown files or links.
  • Use updated antivirus software.
  • Avoid suspicious websites and programs.
